How to access the Pirate Bay without problems

Are you looking for a book, a song, a video or other types of information and found that unless you pay for it, you cannot have it? The Pirate Bay is the site for you. It is a peer-to- peer file sharing web app that lets you download and upload content.

Here you will learn what the Pirate Bay is, how it works, and how to access it. Or if you prefer, you will also see how to use alternative sites that offer similar functionality.

So, let’s start

What is the Pirate Bay?

The Pirate Bay, usually abbreviated TPB, is a peer-to-peer (P2P) web site that allows users to download files, using the BitTorrent protocol. It was created by three Swedes named Peter Sunde, Fredrik Neij and Gottfrid Svartholm, who supported the free sharing of knowledge. In 2009, the three founders were sentenced to a one year in prison and to the payment of a fine.

TBP is free to all, requires no user identification and is available at https://thepiratebay.org/ . However, you will need a Torrent program, such as BitLord, in order to download a file.

TPB censorship and its circumvention

The web site has been subjected to censorship, and it has been blocked in several countries. This has led to measures aimed at circumventing the censorship, by people concerned with the sharing of knowledge, and who agree that no one should be denied the access to sources of knowledge.

There are several techniques used to circumvent censorship. One of them is site mirroring, which basically consist of creating a copy of the whole site and offering it to the public with a different url. Another technology is the use of a proxy, which is a server that acts as an intermediary for accessing the TPB server. And finally, there is the use of Virtual Private Networks or VPNs, which are networks with access restricted to members, and which are attached to the public Internet.

The main problem is that they are varying quite frequently, because they may also be subject to censorship or due to the fact that the volunteer site stops working due to lack of funding or other reasons. Using VPNs

Virtual Private Networks or VPNs extend private networks by connecting them to the Internet. Thus, they give you privacy and security at the same time, help you access content that maybe blocked in your country, and allow you to appear to be in another country. They also stop authorities from tracking your internet activities. In other words, VPNs allow you to stay anonymous.

This technology works by encrypting your connection and using a server where your IP address is replaced by another one. VPNs are widely used in highly censored countries such as China, and in countries where surveillance laws are very invasive of your privacy, such as the USA, UK and Australia. There are some VPNs where you need to pay a fee. Others are for free.

But, remember, when choosing a VPN for yourself, do your research and compare the different services. PC Magazine offers reliable studies. Wikipedia can also help, as it provides important information.

And, if you fancy to create your own VPN, you can use OpenVPN, an open source software available for free.

Alternatives to the Pirate Bay

Another way to acquire files is to use alternative Torrent sites. There are many of them, which offer an alternative to TPB. However, they have also been subjected to censorship and thus, some of them disappear over time. Thus, you need to check updated lists, and you can also consult lists of proxy servers. At present, some of the most popular ones are:

  • 1337x: it is a free P2P file sharing site, using the Torrent protocol, which provides a list of torrent files and magnet links. The site has been banned by Google, and does not show in search lists. The site also presents a list of alternative domains.

  • Kickass (revived) launched in 2008, it was a free P2P file sharing site that was closed, together with its proxy servers in 2016. The site was revived in the same year.

  • RARBG: also launched in 2008, it offers torrent files and magnet links to facilitate file sharing. It has been blocked in a few countries.

  • Torrents.me: lets you search in multiple torrent sites. The site has a Facebook page.

A word of caution

Torrent sites have been the target of criminals and people spreading malware. Beware of this fact and take precautions. Also, be careful of files offered via Facebook and Google groups. In many cases they have been used by scammers.
